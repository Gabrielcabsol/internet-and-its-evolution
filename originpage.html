The First Creators

The internet began in the late 1960s with ARPANET, a research project by the U.S. Department of Defense designed to link computers at various institutions. The development of TCP/IP protocols in the 1980s standardized network communication, leading to the expansion of ARPANET into the modern internet. Tim Berners-Lee's introduction of the World Wide Web in 1991 made the internet accessible to the public, enabling easy navigation and information sharing. The internet has since grown into a global network supporting a wide range of services and applications.

After the internet was created in the U.S., several major changes occurred:

Communication: Instant, global communication through email, online forums, and social media.

Information Access: Immediate access to vast amounts of information via online resources and search engines.

Economy: Rise of e-commerce, altering business practices and enabling global sales.

Media: Transformation of traditional media with the growth of online news and streaming services.

Work and Education: Increased viability of remote work and online learning.

Culture and Society: Global sharing and access to diverse cultural content.

Components

When the internet became available to everyone, some important components were given to the market.

Routers and Switches:

Routers:

Cisco 2500 Series: One of the first widely used routers in the 1990s, designed for enterprise environments.

Netgear DG834: A home router that combined router and modem functionality into a single device, released in the early 2000s.

Switches:

Cisco Catalyst 5000 Series: Introduced in the 1990s, it was one of the first switches designed for high-performance networks.

3Com SuperStack Series: Launched in the 1990s, it provided switching solutions for enterprise-level networks.

Servers:

IBM System/360: Introduced in 1964, it was one of the first mainframe systems to establish a universal computing architecture.

DEC PDP-11: Released in 1970, a minicomputer used for both server tasks and data processing.

Modems:

Hayes Smartmodem 300: Introduced in 1981, it was one of the first modems to become an industry standard for data communication over telephone lines.

U.S. Robotics Courier: A popular modem in the 1980s and 1990s, known for its reliability in high-speed connections for its time.
